The household that never formed

94 million households own a pet. The fertility rate is 1.6 and falling. These are not competing trends. They are the same trend: a fundamental recomposition of the American household that the apartment industry has noticed in its marketing materials but not yet absorbed into its capital allocation, site selection, unit design, or operating model.

The apartment was designed for a family.
The family is not coming.
The fur baby is already here.

This thesis is strongest in urban core and Class A product where the renter base skews younger and childless. Pet ownership is rising across every demographic; the question is how fast it reaches your submarket.
